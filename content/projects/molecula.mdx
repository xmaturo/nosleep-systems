## The Problem

Ride comfort in autonomous vehicles is a control problem: the MPC controller must balance trajectory tracking, safety, and passenger comfort simultaneously. The standard approach is to hand-tune the cost function weights — a process with no theoretical justification that restarts every time vehicle dynamics change.

## What It Does

MOLECULA is an LTV-MPC architecture where the cost function weights are derived from the Glue Program's crossover law. The connection is not metaphorical — there is a formal derivation chain from the universal constant ξ = ½ to a specific weight schedule that governs how the controller prioritizes competing objectives as driving conditions change.

The weight schedule is not static. It evolves with driving conditions, following the same saturation dynamics that the Glue Program proves are universal for oscillatory systems under detailed balance.

## Why This Matters

This is a theory-to-product pipeline:

1. **The Glue Program** proves a universal constant governing oscillatory phase dynamics
2. **MOLECULA** turns that constant into a principled weight schedule for an MPC controller
3. **The AV product** uses that controller for ride comfort optimization

The weights are not approximations of the theory. They are direct consequences of it. The derivation is in the paper.
